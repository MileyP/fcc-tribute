Yosemite

Granite cliffs, waterfalls, clear streams, giant sequoia groves, lakes, mountains, and glaciers make up this World Heritage Site in the Sierra Nevada mountains of California. It no wonder that Yosemite is one of the most-visited parks in the United States.

Hiking

With over 800 miles of trails, you can probably find somewhere to hike. From a quick trip on the ultra-scenic Mist Trail, to the all-day backcountry adventure of Tenaya Canyon, to multi-day backpacking loops in the high-sierra, there is something for everyone.

Climbing

It only takes a few minutes standing in El Cap meadow to understand why the granite walls of Yosemite were key in the development of modern rock climbing. In addition to the big walls of El Capitan and Half Dome, you can find climbers hanging out at smaller cliffs like Reed's Pinnacle and scattered Boulders on the valley floor.

Winter

The fun doesn't stop when the crowds head for warmer climates. Snowshoe to Dewey Point for jaw-dropping views of the valley, or ski all the way to the Ostrander Hut and make some new friends.
